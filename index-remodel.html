Radiologic technologists take x-rays and CAT scans, administer non-radioactive material into a patient’s blood stream, and interpret the images that are produced.

They may specialize in an area like geriatrics or pediatrics, or they make work with ultrasound, magnetic resonance, or computed tomography.

As the population ages, the demand for radiologic technologists will increase, making this a highly desirable career path.
